# MY COMPANY

Reley Network. Current as of Q2 2026. Update when priorities shift, not on a schedule.

## Goals

This year (2026):

1. **Onboard 1,000–5,000 paying agents/devs to mainnet.** This is the headline number. Anything below ~1k means we don't have PMF yet. Anything in this range is a clear win.
2. **Land at least one integration with a major agent project.** Real partnership where another well-known agent team builds on Reley primitives. One is enough to validate the protocol.
3. **Close a funding round.** Size and stage TBD; the round exists to extend runway through PMF, not to scale prematurely.
4. **Build founder presence in agent x crypto.** Recognized voice in the space, not a ghost.

Distribution channels that matter for hitting these goals:
- X / Crypto Twitter (founder presence + discourse)
- Solana ecosystem: foundation, hackathons, devrel
- AI agent communities (ElizaOS, Virtuals, similar)

## Focus right now

Q2 2026 priority order:

1. **Onboarding early agents/devs to mainnet.** This is #1. Hand-holding the first real users, fixing what breaks, removing friction.
2. Shipping core protocol features (continues in parallel because onboarding surfaces gaps).
3. Fundraising conversations and content/founder presence run alongside but don't displace the top two.

If a task doesn't push toward more agents on mainnet OR close the round OR build credibility, it's probably not the work for this quarter.

## Saying no to

- **Multi-chain expansion.** No EVM, no Sui, no Move-anything. Solana only. The agent-economy thesis depends on Solana's speed and cost; any multi-chain work right now is a distraction dressed as opportunity.
- **Launching a token before the product is ready.** Token-first plays are the thing I publicly disrespect. Reley's economy mechanism ships when the protocol earns it, not before.
- **Hiring or building a big team.** Staying solo + AI-augmented. The bet is that this beats a 10–20 person crypto team for the work that matters this year.
- Human-facing consumer UX is deprioritized but not banned. Agents are the user; humans are downstream.

## The contrarian bets

Two beliefs most peers in agent x crypto don't share. Use these as a tiebreaker when a decision is ambiguous:

1. **Reputation, not identity, is the core primitive.** Most of the space fixates on identity (who an agent is). The actual unlock is reputation (what an agent has done). Design choices that strengthen reputation over identity win the tie.
2. **Agents pay for their own infra. No human in the loop.** Pure agent-to-agent economy. If a feature requires a human to sign or approve a routine txn, it's the wrong shape.
